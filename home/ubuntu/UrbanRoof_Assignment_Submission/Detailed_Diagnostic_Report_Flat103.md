# Detailed Diagnostic Report (DDR)
**Property:** Flat No. 103  
**Inspection Date:** 27th September 2022  
**Report ID:** UR-DDR-2022-103  

---

## 1. Property Issue Summary
Flat No. 103 is currently experiencing extensive moisture-related issues, primarily manifesting as capillary dampness at the skirting levels of the Hall, Common Bedroom, Master Bedroom, and Kitchen. The investigation reveals that the moisture ingress is largely driven by waterproofing failures in the wet areas (Common Bathroom and Master Bedroom Bathroom) and exacerbated by structural cracks in the external walls. The seepage has progressed to the point of affecting the parking ceiling below the flat, indicating a high volume of water transit through the floor slab.

## 3. Probable Root Cause
The diagnostic analysis identifies three primary root causes:
1.  **Waterproofing Failure:** Open tile joints and failed seals around Nahani traps in the bathrooms are allowing water to penetrate the brickbat coba.
2.  **Capillary Action:** Water trapped in the floor substrate is migrating horizontally and rising up the internal walls via capillary action, manifesting at the skirting levels.
3.  **External Ingress:** Structural cracks in the external walls and leaking external pipes are allowing rainwater to enter the building envelope.

---

## 4. Severity Assessment
- **Severity:** **HIGH**
- **Reasoning:** The moisture ingress is widespread and has begun to cause secondary damage such as efflorescence and paint failure. The fact that seepage is visible in the parking area below indicates that the floor slab is saturated, which could eventually lead to reinforcement corrosion if not addressed immediately.

---

## 5. Recommended Actions
1.  **Regrouting:** All bathroom floor and wall tile joints should be cleaned and regrouted with high-performance epoxy grout.
2.  **Trap Sealing:** Ensure all Nahani traps are properly sealed and grouted.
3.  **External Repair:** Fill all external wall cracks with appropriate sealants and repair leaking external plumbing lines.
4.  **Internal Restoration:** After the leaks are stopped and the walls have fully dried (monitored via thermal imaging), the internal surfaces should be scraped, treated for salts, and repainted.
